The Capital Gap

Deals get
discipline.
The capital
around them
doesn't.

Most private equity and real estate sponsors apply extraordinary rigor to underwriting individual assets. Reserve policies, hold periods, return thresholds — all scrutinized.

But the cash sitting between transactions? It drifts. It earns money-market rates — or worse, nothing. It bleeds quietly while attention focuses elsewhere.

That is the treasury gap. And at scale, it is not a rounding error.

01

The Liquidity Trap

Capital earmarked for deployment sits in low-yield instruments by default — not by design. Sponsors assume liquidity requires a yield penalty. It doesn't have to.

02

The Scale Illusion

A 2% yield difference feels abstract until it compounds across $50M, $100M, or $250M of idle capital. Then it becomes a material line item with a name on it.

03

The Invisible Cost

No invoice arrives for the yield you didn't earn. No LP report flags it as a loss. That invisibility makes it persistent — and makes solving it look like low priority.
